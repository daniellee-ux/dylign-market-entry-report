Barcode generator FAQ

The questions growing brands ask before they print a barcode on a label.

Is this barcode generator really free?

Yes — free, no account, no watermark and no limit on how many you make. Generate a barcode, download the SVG and use it on your own labels or products however you like.

What’s the difference between Code 128 and UPC?

Code 128 encodes letters, numbers and symbols, so it’s ideal for internal SKUs, lot codes and short URLs. UPC-A is the fixed 12-digit numeric code retailers scan at checkout — you need a GS1-assigned number to sell in most stores. Use Code 128 for your own tracking, UPC-A when a retailer requires it.

Will these barcodes actually scan?

They’re built to the Code 128 and UPC-A specs, complete with the correct check digit and the quiet-zone margins scanners need. Print them dark on a light background at a sensible size and they read cleanly. We also verify the scan on your free proof before production.

Can I print these on my labels?

Absolutely — that’s the whole point. Download the SVG, place it in your label design, and order it on our roll labels from 100 units. The waterproof BOPP film keeps bars sharp and scannable on bottles, jars and freezer packs.
